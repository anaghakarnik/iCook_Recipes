gulab jamun

A famous Indian dessert typically known as 'mithai' that is also declared as the national dessert of India.

Recipe Description

Soft dough made mainly from milk, shaped in balls, deep fried in oil, and then finally soaked in light sugar syrup that is flavored with cardamom, saffron, and rose water.

prep time

20 min.
cook time

45 min.
servings

Serves 10 - 2pc/pers
ingredients
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 3 tbsp semolina/rava
  • 2-3 tbsp ghee/clarified butter
  • 2-3 tbsp milk
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (might not need all)
  • 2-3 cardamom pods
  • 3-5 saffron strands
  • 1/2 tbsp lime juice
  • 6 cups oil (depends on the container used for deep-frying)
  • 1 tbsp rose water (optional)
instructions

step 1

To make the dough, mix together the condensed milk, milk, semolina, baking soda, and ghee. Then add the flour slowly by parts (1 tbsp at a time and mix) until it turns into a soft dough. If the dough is too wet add more flour. Once the dough is ready, divide it equally and then shape it into medium sized balls.

step 2

Heat oil lightly in a deep-frying pan. Keep the stove on a low temperature and then fry the dough balls about 3 to 4 at a time. Lightly keep moving the balls in the oil in a circular motion using a stariner ladle to cook all the sides evenly. Cook the balls until they are evenly brown on all sides.

step 3

To make the sugar syrup, boil together the water, sugar, cardamom (crush the pods and make a fine powder), and saffron. The consistency of the syrup should not be too thick or too watery. When the color of the syrup will turn a faint yellow, add the lime juice and turn off the stove after another boil.

step 4

When the sugar syrup is ready, add the fried dough balls in the syrup and soak them for about two hours before serving. Serve the gulab jamun warm or cold and enjoy!
